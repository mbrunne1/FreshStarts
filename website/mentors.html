Why Mentorship Matters

Career change can feel isolating. You're leaving behind colleagues who understand your work, entering industries where you feel like a beginner. Our mentors have been exactly where you are.

What our mentors provide:

  • Honest advice about what the transition is really like
  • Resume and interview guidance specific to your target industry
  • Networking introductions and connections
  • Emotional support during the uncertain phases
  • Reality checks on timeline, salary expectations, and training needs

Sarah Martinez

Elementary Teacher → Corporate Training Manager

📍 Location: Atlanta, GA (Available for video calls nationwide)

🎂 Age at Transition: 38

⏱️ Transition Timeline: 8 months (while still teaching)

💰 Salary Change: +40% increase

Her Story

After 15 years in elementary education, Sarah felt burned out by increasing class sizes and decreasing support. She loved teaching but couldn't see herself doing it for another 20 years. Through FreshStart's Skills Translator, she realized her classroom management, curriculum design, and presentation skills translated perfectly to corporate training.

She spent 8 months building a portfolio of training materials, earning a certification in instructional design, and networking with corporate trainers. She kept teaching while transitioning, which made the financial risk manageable. Now she develops and delivers training programs for a Fortune 500 tech company—and earns significantly more with better work-life balance.

What She Can Help You With

  • Translating teaching experience for corporate roles
  • Building a training portfolio while employed
  • Networking in the L&D (Learning & Development) field
  • Managing the emotional aspects of leaving education
  • Salary negotiation for career changers

💬 Sarah's Advice: "Don't wait until you're completely burned out. Start exploring while you still have energy. And remember—your teaching skills are incredibly valuable outside the classroom. You just need to learn how to talk about them differently."

James Kowalski

Retail Store Manager → Software Sales Executive

📍 Location: Chicago, IL (Available for video calls nationwide)

🎂 Age at Transition: 42

⏱️ Transition Timeline: 6 months

💰 Salary Change: +55% increase (with commission potential)

His Story

James spent 18 years climbing the retail management ladder—from sales associate to store manager to district supervisor. The long hours, weekend work, and physical demands were wearing him down, especially as he approached his mid-40s. He wanted a career with growth potential that didn't require him to miss his kids' soccer games.

A friend suggested he look into tech sales, pointing out that his customer service skills, ability to hit targets, and team leadership experience were exactly what software companies valued. James was skeptical—he'd never worked in tech and didn't have a degree. But after researching and talking to mentors on FreshStart, he realized his objections were just fear.

He took a three-month sales bootcamp (while working retail), built relationships with tech sales recruiters on LinkedIn, and landed an SDR (Sales Development Representative) role at a growing SaaS company. Within two years, he was promoted to Account Executive, earning more than he ever did in retail—with weekends off.

What He Can Help You With

  • Breaking into tech without a technical background
  • Translating customer service/retail skills for corporate roles
  • Overcoming imposter syndrome as an older career changer
  • Navigating tech sales bootcamps and certifications
  • LinkedIn networking strategies that actually work

💬 James's Advice: "Age and lack of tech experience aren't dealbreakers—they're stories you need to reframe. Companies want people who can sell, build relationships, and hit goals. If you've done that in retail, you can do it in tech."

Lisa Ramirez

Registered Nurse → Healthcare Consultant

📍 Location: Denver, CO (Available for video calls nationwide)

🎂 Age at Transition: 35

⏱️ Transition Timeline: 12 months

💰 Salary Change: +35% increase + flexible schedule

Her Story

Lisa loved patient care but was exhausted by the 12-hour shifts, weekend rotations, and emotional toll of hospital nursing. After 10 years in critical care, she wanted to use her healthcare expertise in a way that didn't destroy her physical and mental health.

She discovered healthcare consulting—helping hospitals and clinics improve patient outcomes, streamline workflows, and implement new technologies. Her clinical experience was exactly what consulting firms valued, but she needed to learn the business side of healthcare.

She earned an online certificate in healthcare administration while still working as a nurse (her employer's tuition reimbursement covered most of it). She networked with healthcare consultants, shadowed a project, and eventually landed a role at a mid-sized consulting firm. Now she uses her clinical knowledge to solve system-level problems—with a M-F schedule and no night shifts.

What She Can Help You With

  • Transitioning from clinical to administrative healthcare roles
  • Leveraging tuition reimbursement for career change training
  • Building consulting skills while still in patient care
  • Work-life balance strategies during transition
  • Understanding the healthcare consulting industry

💬 Lisa's Advice: "Your clinical experience is gold. Consultants with real bedside experience are rare and highly valued. Don't think you need an MBA—your patient care background is the credential that matters."
